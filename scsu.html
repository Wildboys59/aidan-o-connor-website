St Cloud State Huskies

The St Cloud State Huskies Hockey Program was founded in 1934.

The St. Cloud State Huskies men's hockey program, has a rich history in collegiate ice hockey. Originally a member of the NAIA, the team transitioned to NCAA Division II in the 1960s and then moved to Division I in the 1980s. Over the years, the Huskies have built a strong reputation in the NCAA, consistently competing in the Western Collegiate Hockey Association (WCHA) and later in the National Collegiate Hockey Conference (NCHC). The program gained prominence in the 1990s and 2000s, with multiple NCAA tournament appearances. The Huskies achieved significant milestones, including their first Frozen Four appearance in 2013, and reached the championship game in 2021. With a commitment to developing talent, St. Cloud State has produced numerous NHL players and has a dedicated fan base, making it a key player in college hockey.
